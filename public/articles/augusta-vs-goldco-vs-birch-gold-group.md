## Fees Deep-Dive: What You Actually Pay

Understanding the full fee stack is essential before choosing any Gold IRA provider. There are four distinct fee layers, and it's common for first-time investors to focus only on the first one.

### 1. IRA Setup Fee (One-Time)
A one-time charge to establish the self-directed IRA with the custodian. Typically $50–$100. All three companies in this guide charge approximately $50.[^5][^7][^8]

### 2. Annual Custodian / Administrative Fee
Paid to the third-party custodian (not the Gold IRA dealer) for account administration, recordkeeping, tax reporting (Form 1099-R), and IRS compliance. Ranges from $75–$125/year depending on custodian. Augusta charges $125, Goldco's recommended custodian charges $80, Birch charges $125.

### 3. Annual Storage Fee
Paid to the IRS-approved depository for vaulting your metals. Ranges from $100–$300 depending on whether you choose:
- **Non-segregated (commingled) storage:** Your metals are pooled with others' in the same vault, though fully accounted for and insured. Typically $100–$110/year.
- **Segregated storage:** Your specific coins and bars are physically separated and individually identified. Typically $150–$300/year. Recommended for larger accounts where the value of knowing your specific metals are isolated outweighs the additional cost.

### 4. Coin Premiums (The Most Significant Variable)
This is the fee most investors underestimate. When you [buying gold online](/article/how-to-buy-gold-online-safely) coins or bars for your IRA, you pay a **premium above the spot price** — the current market price per troy ounce. This premium reflects dealer markup, minting costs, and distribution.

Typical premiums in 2026:
- Gold bullion bars: ~1%–3% above spot
- American Gold Eagle coins: ~3%–8% above spot (varies by dealer and market conditions)
- Premium coins / numismatics: often 20%–100%+ above spot — **never appropriate for a retirement account**

**Before funding any Gold IRA account:** Request an itemized written quote showing the specific coins or bars you will purchase, the current spot price, and the exact premium/spread you are being charged. This is the single most important piece of information you can obtain. A company that refuses to provide it in writing is not operating transparently.[^9]

## IRS Rules Every Gold IRA Buyer Must Know

A Gold IRA is a **self-directed IRA** — a legitimate, IRS-recognized account structure governed by the Internal Revenue Code. However, several rules are non-negotiable, and violations carry severe tax consequences.

### Purity Standards — What Metal Qualifies

The IRS mandates minimum purity standards for each precious metal under IRC Section 408(m)(3):[^10]

| Metal | Minimum Purity Required | Key Exception |
|---|---|---|
| Gold | 99.5% (.995 fine) | American Gold Eagle (91.67%) is specifically authorized by Congress despite lower purity |
| Silver | 99.9% (.999 fine) | None |
| Platinum | 99.95% (.9995 fine) | None |
| Palladium | 99.95% (.9995 fine) | None |

Any gold product that fails to meet .995 purity — other than the American Gold Eagle — is automatically disqualified from IRA inclusion.

### IRS-Approved Coins and Bars

**Eligible gold coins:**
- American Gold Eagle (the only coin exempt from the .995 purity rule)
- American Gold Buffalo (.9999 pure)
- Canadian Gold Maple Leaf (.9999)
- Austrian Gold Philharmonic (.9999)
- Australian Gold Kangaroo/Nugget (.9999)

**Eligible gold bars:**
- Must be produced by a refiner accredited by NYMEX, COMEX, or the London Bullion Market Association (LBMA)
- Must carry the refiner's name, purity, and weight markings

**Prohibited for IRA inclusion:**
- Collectible or numismatic coins — regardless of gold content
- Pre-1933 gold coins (Morgan dollars, Double Eagles, etc.)
- South African Krugerrands (below .995 purity — unlike Eagles, they have no Congressional exception)
- Jewelry, gold rounds from non-accredited sources, or any damaged/uncertified products

> **Red flag:** Any Gold IRA company that recommends numismatic, collectible, or "rare" coins for your IRA account is acting against your interests. These coins carry enormous premiums that make them effectively impossible to sell at fair value in a retirement context. All three companies reviewed here focus on investment-grade bullion — which is the correct approach.[^11]

### The Home Storage Prohibition

You **cannot** [store gold at home ](/article/how-to-store-gold-at-home-safely), or any other IRA precious metals, in a personal safe, or in a bank safe deposit box. This is one of the most commonly misunderstood rules in the precious metals IRA space.

Under IRS rules and IRC Section 4975, if you take personal possession of your IRA metals, the entire IRA is treated as a taxable distribution in that year. You will owe income tax on the full value plus a 10% early withdrawal penalty if you are under age 59½. So-called "home storage IRA" schemes that claim otherwise are illegal and will trigger IRS enforcement.[^10]

**All IRA metals must be:**
1. Purchased through the custodian after the account is established
2. Shipped directly from the dealer to an IRS-approved depository
3. Held in the custody of the depository — never in the investor's physical possession while in IRA status

### Custodian Requirement

A Gold IRA must be administered by an IRS-approved custodian — a bank, trust company, or other IRS-designated non-bank trustee. Standard brokerage firms (Fidelity, [Fidelity, Vanguard, and Schwab](/article/fidelity-vs-vanguard-vs-schwab), Vanguard) do not serve as precious metals IRA custodians. The custodian is responsible for recordkeeping, tax reporting, RMD calculations, and ensuring compliance.

The Gold IRA companies in this guide (Augusta, Goldco, Birch Gold) are **dealers**, not custodians. They source and facilitate the purchase of metals. Your custodian (Equity Trust, STRATA Trust, GoldStar Trust) holds the account and manages the legal/tax compliance.

## FAQ

### Which Gold IRA company has the lowest all-in fees?

On the basis of published annual fees, **Goldco** is marginally lower in Year 2 onward ($180/year non-segregated) versus Birch Gold (~$225–$235) and Augusta (~$225). However, fees are only one dimension of cost — the coin premium (markup above spot price) is often a larger variable, particularly for investors purchasing American Gold Eagles or proof coins. Request itemized written quotes from any company before deciding, and calculate total cost including premiums, not just the custodian and storage fees.[^9]

### Can I store Gold IRA coins at home?

No. Home storage of IRA metals is a prohibited transaction under IRS rules and IRC Section 4975. The IRS treats personal possession of IRA metals as an immediate taxable distribution — triggering income tax on the full account value plus a 10% penalty if you are under 59½. Any company or promoter claiming there is a "home storage IRA" exemption is providing false information. All three companies reviewed here correctly direct IRA customers to IRS-approved depositories.[^10]

### What is the average historical return on a Gold IRA?

Gold does not pay dividends and does not compound like equity investments. Its return is the change in the market price of gold. Over the long term, gold has historically served as an **inflation hedge and store of value** rather than a growth asset. For reference: gold's price climbed from approximately $2,600/oz in early 2025 to over $3,000/oz in early 2026 — a significant appreciation driven by inflation concerns, geopolitical uncertainty, and dollar weakness.[^12] However, gold also went essentially flat from 2012 to 2018, underperforming stocks significantly during that period. Most financial advisors recommend treating gold as a portfolio **hedge** representing 5–15% of retirement assets — not as a primary growth vehicle.

### How do I roll over an existing 401(k) or IRA to a Gold IRA?

The process is straightforward but requires following IRS transfer rules carefully:

1. **Choose your Gold IRA company** (Augusta, Goldco, or Birch Gold) and open a self-directed IRA with their recommended custodian — takes 1–3 business days
2. **Initiate a direct rollover or trustee-to-trustee transfer** from your existing 401(k) or IRA to the new self-directed IRA — a direct transfer does not count against your annual contribution limit and avoids any withholding or penalties
3. **Select your IRS-approved metals** with guidance from your specialist — the company handles the purchase coordination
4. **Metals are purchased by the custodian** and shipped directly to the IRS-approved depository — you never take possession
5. **Receive account confirmation** from the custodian showing your holdings

The full process typically takes 1–2 weeks from account opening to funded and invested. Note: you cannot transfer [physical gold](/article/how-to-buy-physical-gold-online-safely) you already own into a Gold IRA — all metals must be newly purchased through the custodian.[^13]
